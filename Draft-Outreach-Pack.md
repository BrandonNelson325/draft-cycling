## Why this is move #1

One video from the right creator = thousands of the exact buyers you want, and it's free. But reviewers get pitched constantly, so three rules decide whether you get ignored:

1. **Lead with what's genuinely new** (GPX→plan, chat-with-coach, $9.99 vs $200 coach). Reviewers only cover things that give their audience something to react to.
2. **Make it zero-effort for them.** Free lifetime account, a 60-second summary, no "hop on a call." They're busy solo operators.
3. **Don't ask for a review.** Ask them to *try it* and give feedback. The review is their idea, not your ask. This flips the dynamic and dramatically raises reply rates.

**Before you send anything:** create a clean **press/reviewer promo code** (free lifetime) and have your site show a 20-second "what makes it different" section. If a reviewer clicks through to a confusing page, you've wasted the shot.

## The "seeding kit" (attach/link in every pitch)

Have these ready so a curious reviewer never has to dig:

- **A 3-bullet "what's different" blurb** (GPX plans · chat coach · $9.99 vs $200 coach).
- **One 30–60s screen-recording** showing the GPX-to-plan flow. This single clip does more than any paragraph — the "drop a route, get a plan" moment is your whole pitch, visually.
